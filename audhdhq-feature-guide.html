Feature Guide

How AuDHD HQ
actually works.

A detailed reference for every tool in the system — what each one does, what data it stores, how the pieces connect, and what to use when you're in different brain states.

audhd-hq.web.app · Feature Guide v2 · Phase 6 complete · 29 tools documented
Overview

How the system works

AuDHD HQ is a modular system — each tool is a standalone page, but they share data and signal each other through a common storage layer. Here's the architecture in plain terms.

🏗️
The Architecture
Storage · Signals · Shared Shell
Storage (hq-store.js)
All data lives in your browser's localStorage. Every tool reads and writes using the HQStore layer — a key registry that prevents typos and manages storage quota automatically. If storage gets above 80% full, it auto-evicts old check-in entries. If it hits 90%, you'll see a warning toast.
Signals (HQBus)
Tools communicate via a BroadcastChannel bus. When you save something in one tab, other open tabs update automatically. Channels include: db-signal (DayBuilder data changed), cascade-signal (monthly/weekly cascade), flags-updated, energy-updated.
Shell (hq-core.js + hq-shell.css)
Every page loads the same core shell, which injects the sidenav, topbar, and theme system. Themes shift automatically based on time of day (morning / afternoon / night) with smooth opacity transitions. You can override this in Customize.
💜
Key principle: Nothing is sent to a server. All 29 tools share one localStorage space on your device. Export regularly via Data Sync to keep a backup.

🌅 Daily Tools

Daily tools

These are the tools you'll use most often — ideally once a day, though they're built to be forgiving when you miss days.

✅
Check-In checkin.html
Mood · Energy · Body · Sleep · Workday · AM Checklist · Capture Panel · Weekly/Monthly summary
▼
What it does

The Check-In is the heartbeat of AuDHD HQ. It's a structured daily self-assessment that takes under two minutes. You log your mood (Blah/Fine/Good/Great), physical energy, emotional/cognitive energy, body sensations, sleep hours and quality, workday rating, miles walked, and a free-text note. Once a week (or month), it also prompts for a broader review — both can be triggered manually at any time.

Your entries build over time and feed into the Health Tracker's trend graphs, the home screen's capacity bar and metrics strip, and the Provider Report export.

Key features
  • Day streak counter — tracks consecutive days you've checked in
  • Body chips split into Physical and Emotional sections — rate each independently; sensory and anxiety chips include a "Not Applicable" option
  • AM Morning Checklist — meds taken, teeth brushed, cat fed, water; only visible in the morning time slot
  • Meds logging — mark medications taken, links to Health Tracker meds tab
  • Capture Panel (Notes) — type selector lets you tag a note as Win / Worry / Note / Reminder; route chips send it to Thought Jar, Weekly inbox, Monthly inbox, or Health notes; everything passes through Thought Jar first
  • Route to: — route a thought directly to Thought Jar, Worry Time, or Wins from within the check-in
  • Recent check-ins — scrollable history at the bottom
  • Weekly review — an extra section on Sundays (or your chosen day) for week-level reflection; can now be triggered manually any day (shows dashed border when in manual mode)
  • Monthly review — similarly unlocked; both weekly and monthly triggers no longer locked to specific calendar positions
Connects to
Health Tracker (trends) Home screen (capacity, streak) Global Data Provider Report Thought Jar
💡
You don't have to fill in every field every day. Even a partial check-in (just mood + energy) is useful and counts toward your streak.
🫙
Thought Jar (Brain Dump) brain-dump.html
Capture anything — route it anywhere — clear your head fast
▼
What it does

A zero-friction capture tool. When something is rattling around your head — a task, a worry, an idea, an observation — you dump it here. The Thought Jar doesn't judge, organize, or require categories. You just write. Later (when you have capacity), you can route entries to the right place.

Key features
  • One-tap entry — just type and hit add
  • Entry types: Task, Worry, Idea, Vent, Win, Note
  • Route to: send an entry to Taskboard, Worry Time, Wins, Idea Studio, or just archive it
  • Badge on home screen shows how many unprocessed entries you have
  • Full history with search and filter by type
Connects to
Taskboard Idea Studio Home screen badge
📅
Timeline day-view.html
Read-only day view · Recurring events · All-day banner
▼
What it does

A visual, read-only view of your day. The Timeline pulls events from the Monthly Planner (including recurring events) and displays them in chronological order. It's the "what's happening today" view — not a place to edit, just to see.

Key features
  • Today's events pulled from Monthly Planner
  • Recurring events appear automatically (daily, weekly, monthly, yearly)
  • All-day events pinned to a banner at the top
  • Time-based positioning on a visual timeline
  • Navigate forward/backward to other days
Connects to
Monthly Planner (event source) Recurring Events system
🗓
DayBuilder day-view.html#plan
Visual block schedule · Time-slot planning · Build your day intentionally
▼
What it does

Where the Timeline is read-only, DayBuilder is interactive. You build your day in time blocks — assigning tasks, breaks, appointments, and buffer time to specific slots. It's a visual block planner that makes time feel tangible rather than abstract.

Key features
  • Drag-and-resize time blocks across a visual day grid
  • Block types: Focus, Break, Admin, Appointment, Buffer, Free
  • Pull tasks from Taskboard directly into the day
  • Signals changes to other open tabs via db-signal bus
Connects to
Taskboard Home screen quick access
🔁
Routines + Prepwork routines-prepwork.html
Morning · Night prep · End-of-day · Custom routines
▼
What it does

Structured checklists for your recurring routines. AuDHD brains often need externalized sequences for things that neurotypical people do "automatically" — morning prep, night wind-down, getting-out-of-the-house. This tool holds those sequences so you don't have to hold them in your head.

Key features
  • Built-in routine types: Morning, Night Prep, End-of-Day
  • Customizable steps — add, remove, reorder
  • Check-off mode — tap through your routine step by step
  • Resets daily so it's fresh the next morning
🧠 Core Brains

Core brains

These are the big-topic life management tools. Each one handles a major domain that otherwise takes up background cognitive load.

🗂
Project Brain project-brain.html
Projects · Milestones · Tasks · Timeline bars · Ideas
▼
What it does

A project management tool designed for the AuDHD experience — which means it accommodates non-linear progress, hyperfocus bursts, and the fact that "projects" often start as vague ideas before they have a structure. You can track multiple active projects with milestones, tasks, ideas, and a visual timeline bar.

Key features
  • Projects with title, status, start date, and visual timeline bar
  • Milestones and sub-tasks per project
  • Inline concept title editing
  • Ideas tab — capture project-adjacent thoughts without committing them to tasks
  • Export/import mechanism for individual project data
  • Status options: Active, Paused, Completed, Archived
Connects to
Taskboard (task routing) Idea Studio
💰
Money Brain money-brain.html
Budget · Bills · Income · Savings · Debt · Subscriptions · Transactions
▼
What it does

A comprehensive personal finance manager built specifically to reduce the executive function load of managing money. Money Brain handles the full picture: what's coming in, what's going out, what's due, what you've saved, and where you're in debt. It's particularly useful for irregular income — it has an income-smoothing feature for people who don't get consistent paychecks.

Tabs
  • 📊 Overview — cash flow snapshot, alerts, coming due
  • 📋 Bills — recurring bills, due dates, autopay tracking
  • 🔁 Expenses — recurring expense tracking
  • 💵 Income — income sources and schedule
  • 🎯 Savings — savings goals with progress bars
  • 💳 Transactions — manual transaction log
  • 📺 Subscriptions — subscription tracker with cost summary
  • 💳 Debt — debt accounts, balances, minimum payments
  • 📈 Income Smoothing — average income over time for budgeting
  • 🏦 Finance Setup — configure accounts and preferences
Connects to
Home screen (alerts) Global Data
💡
The cash flow view has three modes: This Month, Next 30 Days, and History. "Next 30 Days" is the one that reduces anxiety — it shows you exactly what's hitting your account before it happens.
🍳
Kitchen Brain kitchen-brain.html
Meal planning · Fridge tracker · Recipes · Grocery list · Receipt cost tracking
▼
What it does

The "what do I eat and what do I have" tool. Kitchen Brain tracks what's in your fridge, helps you plan meals for the week, stores recipes you actually use, and generates a shopping list from your plan. It addresses the classic AuDHD problem of standing in front of the fridge unable to decide, then ordering delivery because planning feels impossible.

Key features
  • Fridge/pantry inventory with expiry date tracking — faster item entry in Phase 6B
  • Weekly meal plan grid (breakfast, lunch, dinner, snacks)
  • Recipe library — save and retrieve what works for you
  • Auto-generated grocery list from the meal plan
  • Safe foods list — things you can always fall back on
  • Receipt/invoice upload foundation — upload grocery receipts to track actual spend by item; groundwork for grocery cost trends
🗂 Planning + Organizing

Planning tools

These tools handle time at different scales — from this month's big goals down to today's task list. They're designed to cascade: set a goal once at the top level and it flows down.

📋
Taskboard taskboard.html
Daily · Weekly · Monthly lanes · Priority flags · Tags
▼
What it does

The central task management hub. Tasks live in three lanes by time horizon: Daily, Weekly, Monthly. Within each lane, tasks can be prioritized, tagged, and flagged urgent. Your Priority Stack on the home screen pulls from here automatically, surfacing your most important uncomplete tasks without you having to open the Taskboard.

Key features
  • Three lanes: Today, This Week, This Month
  • Priority flags (High/Medium/Low) and urgent flag
  • Custom tags from your tag library (configured in Customize)
  • Subtasks within each task
  • Archive completed tasks rather than permanently deleting
  • Drag to reorder within a lane
  • Filter by tag, priority, or status
Connects to
Home screen (Priority Stack) DayBuilder Monthly/Weekly Planner (cascade) Flags system
🗓️
Monthly Planner monthly-planner.html
Monthly goals · Appointments · Recurring events · Cascade to weekly
▼
What it does

High-level monthly planning. Set goals for the month, add appointments and events to the calendar, configure recurring events, and cascade goals down to the Weekly Planner. It's the 30,000-foot view of your life in a given month.

Key features
  • Monthly goal list with categories
  • Calendar view with event dots
  • Recurring events: daily, weekly, monthly, yearly, custom frequencies
  • All-day events that pin to the Timeline banner
  • Cascade: push monthly goals to Weekly Planner
  • Events feed into Timeline view automatically
Connects to
Timeline (event source) Weekly Planner (cascade) Home screen (Coming Up)
📆
Weekly Planner weekly-planner.html
Week view · Inbox pull · Review · Focus intentions
▼
What it does

Mid-level weekly planning. The Weekly Planner bridges your month-level goals and your daily tasks. It gives you a structured space to plan the week ahead, review the week that just ended, and set focus intentions for each day. Tasks can be pulled in from the Taskboard inbox or cascaded from the Monthly Planner.

Key features
  • 7-day view with per-day task lists
  • Inbox pull — drag tasks from Taskboard into the week
  • Weekly review tab — what went well, what didn't, what carries forward
  • Focus intentions — one-sentence daily priority
  • Recurring events appear automatically
Connects to
Monthly Planner (cascade source) Taskboard (inbox) Recurring Events
❤️ Health + Wellness

Health + wellness tools

These tools help you track patterns, manage hard states, and build evidence about your own health over time — including data you can share with providers.

🩺
Health Tracker health-tracker.html
Period · Headaches · Sleep · Meds · Energy · Provider Report · Trends · Conditions Setup
▼
What it does

The most comprehensive tool in AuDHD HQ. Health Tracker aggregates data from your daily check-ins and adds dedicated tracking for health-specific patterns. It's organized into tabs, each covering a different health domain. The Provider Report tab generates a clinical summary you can bring to a doctor or therapist.

Tabs
  • 📊 Overview — health summary dashboard
  • 🩸 Period — cycle tracking, flow, symptoms
  • 🧠 Headaches — migraine/headache log with triggers and severity
  • 😴 Sleep — hours, quality, patterns from check-ins
  • 🌙 Dreams — linked to Dream Journal
  • 🩺 Symptoms — custom symptom tracking; symptoms can be flagged to push into your daily Check-In
  • ⚡ Check-ins — full check-in history view
  • 🔋 Energy — energy patterns over time
  • 🏥 Provider Report — exportable clinical summary
  • 💊 Meds — medication list, schedule, log
  • 🩻 Doctor Prep — questions to bring to appointments
  • 📈 Trends — graphs across all tracked metrics
  • ⚙️ Setup — configure conditions, healthcare providers, and medications; the foundation for provider report pre-population and push-to-check-in flags
Connects to
Check-In (data source for all trends) Dream Journal Data Sync (Provider Report export)
🏥
Provider Report: The report pulls from the last 2 weeks, 30 days, or 3 months of data and formats it as a clinical summary. You can select which categories to include: mental/emotional, physical, sleep, and energy. Export from here or from Data Sync → Provider Data Export.

Dream Journal dream-journal.html
Log dreams · Track symbols · Pattern analysis
▼
What it does

A dedicated journal for logging and analyzing dreams. Beyond simple notes, it lets you tag recurring symbols and themes, track emotional tone, and see patterns emerge over time. Dream data also surfaces in the Health Tracker's Sleep and Dreams tabs.

Key features
  • Quick-capture mode for recording dreams immediately on waking
  • Symbol library — build your own dictionary of recurring symbols
  • Emotional tone tags (vivid, anxious, peaceful, strange, etc.)
  • Pattern analysis — which symbols appear most, which emotions correlate
  • Links to Health Tracker's Dreams tab

Walking Tracker walking-tracker.html
Miles log · 2026 annual goal · Heatmap · Pace engine · Intensity zones · Streaks · Quick-add templates
▼
What it does

Tracks walking mileage toward an annual goal. Log a walk with date, distance, duration, and optional notes. The pace engine automatically calculates MPH and min/mile from your entries and places your walk in an intensity zone. The home screen shows a live progress bar toward your 2026 goal, along with how many miles you need per day to stay on track. The heatmap shows which days had activity across the year.

Key features
  • Annual mileage goal (default: 3,100 mi for 2026)
  • Daily log with distance, duration, and notes
  • Pace engine — calculates MPH and min/mile automatically from your entries
  • Walking intensity zones — light / moderate / brisk / vigorous based on pace
  • Walking streak tracking — consecutive active days
  • Quick-add walk templates — save frequent walks (distance + route) for one-tap logging
  • Daily history — scrollable log of past walks with pace and zone displayed
  • Year heatmap — color intensity by miles
  • Pace calculation — miles/day needed to hit goal
  • Pulls walk miles from daily Check-In if entered there
  • Unit toggle: miles or kilometers
  • Weather integration scaffold — ready for live weather data hookup
Connects to
Home screen (walking strip) Check-In (miles field) Global Data

Firebird Protocol firebird-protocol.html
Task initiation · Coping scripts · Trusted people · Breathing · Grounding
▼
What it does

A crisis-adjacent support tool specifically for the "I can't start" state — executive dysfunction, task initiation failure, freeze response. The Firebird Protocol gives you structured steps to break through, including coping scripts, grounding exercises, breathing prompts, and a list of trusted people to contact when you need external support.

Key features
  • Grounding exercises (5-4-3-2-1 and variations)
  • Breathing guides with timed animations
  • Coping scripts — pre-written self-talk for common stuck states
  • Trusted People list — names, phone numbers, notes
  • One-sentence task prompts to get moving on the hardest task
When to use
When you can't start anything Freeze/shutdown approaching Overwhelm + paralysis
🔥
The "Can't start" button on the home screen quick-state strip goes directly here. You never have to navigate when you're already stuck.

Survival Mode survival-mode.html
Floor tasks · Hard days · Migraine protocol · Shutdown support
▼
What it does

For days when functioning normally isn't possible. Survival Mode shows you only the absolute minimum floor tasks — the things that keep you alive and safe without requiring executive function. It also includes a Migraine Protocol tab for when you're in physical pain and can barely look at a screen.

Key features
  • Floor tasks list — pre-configured minimal survival checklist
  • One-sentence "just do this next" prompts
  • Migraine Protocol tab — low-light, minimal text, very simple steps
  • Session summary — log what you managed to do
  • Customizable floor tasks to match your actual needs
When to use
Autistic burnout ADHD paralysis Migraine / chronic pain flare Shutdown or meltdown recovery

▼

An external social memory system. AuDHD brains often struggle with social recall — forgetting what someone told you, not knowing what to say in a situation, losing track of where a relationship stands. Social Brain stores notes about people, pre-written scripts for common social situations, and tracks how much social energy you have left.

Key features
  • People directory with notes, relationship type taxonomy (friend / family / colleague / acquaintance / provider / etc.), and last contact
  • Contact import — bring in contacts from existing sources to seed your directory
  • Social scripts — pre-written responses for tricky situations
  • Social energy gauge — track how depleted you are
  • Upcoming social commitments tracker
  • Recovery time estimates post-social events

Export all · Selective export · Import · Provider Report · Key browser
▼
What it does

The backup and migration center for all your AuDHD HQ data. From here you can export everything to a single .json file, export only specific modules, import a previous backup, browse all your localStorage keys with sizes and statuses, and generate a Provider Report for medical appointments.

Key features
  • Full Export — one .json file with everything; download or copy to clipboard

⚠️

AuDHD design principles in this app

AuDHD HQ was designed with specific principles that distinguish it from generic productivity apps. Understanding these helps you use it more effectively.

🧩
Low friction entry
Every tool prioritizes getting something in over getting it perfect
Partial check-ins still count. You can Brain Dump without categorizing. Tasks can be added with zero metadata. The system is designed to accept whatever you can give and work with it — not reject incomplete data.
🔄
State-aware navigation
Different brain states → different tools
The "I am right now…" strip on the home screen routes you to the right tool for your current state. Overwhelmed → Survival Mode. Can't start → Firebird Protocol. Shutting down → Survival Mode. Actually okay → Check-In. You don't have to figure out which tool to use when you're already struggling.
📊
Externalized pattern recognition
The app notices patterns so you don't have to
Check-in data feeds into trend graphs in Health Tracker. Walking data appears on the home screen without you having to look. The app holds historical context so your brain doesn't have to. This is especially useful for noticing health patterns that are invisible day-to-day but obvious over weeks.
🌊
Forgiving of inconsistency
Missing days doesn't break anything
The streak counter resets, but you can log a backdated check-in. The walking goal adjusts its pace calculation. Plans can be revised. There is no penalty for disappearing for a week and coming back. The system meets you where you are rather than punishing you for where you weren't.
🧠
Every tool was built for a real problem.
If something in AuDHD HQ resonates deeply, that's not an accident. Use what works. The rest will wait.
