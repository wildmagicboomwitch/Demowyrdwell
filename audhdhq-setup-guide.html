01 · What is AuDHD HQ

An external brain, built for how you actually work.

AuDHD HQ is a personal productivity and life-management system designed specifically for people who are autistic, have ADHD, or both. It's not a generic to-do app with extra steps — it's built from the ground up around how AuDHD brains actually operate: non-linear, context-sensitive, prone to overwhelm, and deeply capable when given the right scaffolding.

Everything lives in one place: your schedule, your money, your health patterns, your mood, your tasks, your recipes, your social scripts, your coping tools. The idea is that when your brain can't hold it all, the app holds it for you.

💜
AuDHD HQ uses language that reflects the actual AuDHD experience. You'll see things like "Survival Mode," "Shutting down," and "Can't start" — not because it pathologizes these states, but because naming them accurately is how you find the right tool at the right moment.
02 · Your Data & Privacy

Your data never leaves your device.

Before you start entering anything personal, here's the short version: AuDHD HQ does not have a server. Nothing you type is sent anywhere. There's no account, no sync, no cloud, no database. All your data lives entirely in your own browser on your own device.

🏠
localStorage — Your data, on your device
When you save anything in AuDHD HQ — a check-in, a budget entry, a walk log — it's written to a part of your browser called localStorage. Think of it like a notepad that your browser keeps on your computer or phone. The app can read it back whenever you open the app. Nothing goes to a server.
📄
JSON exports — Your portable backup
You can download all your data at any time as a .json file — a plain text file that's readable by computers (and humans, if you squint). This is your backup. Keep it somewhere safe like a USB drive or cloud folder. You can import it back in at any time to restore everything.
✅
What this means in practice: If you clear your browser's site data, or switch to a different browser or device, your AuDHD HQ data won't automatically follow you. That's why regular exports are important — they're your safety net.

The two things worth understanding

Thing What it is What to do
localStorage Where your data lives in real time. It's always there when you open the app on the same browser. Browser dev tools can view it, but nobody else can access it remotely. Just use the app normally — your data saves automatically as you go. No manual save button required.
.json export A downloadable snapshot of everything. One file, all your data. It's human-readable text (though it looks technical). You can open it in Notepad if you're curious. Go to Data Sync → Export All Data and save the file somewhere outside your browser. Do this regularly — especially before switching devices or clearing browser data.
⚠️
One important heads-up: Each browser and each device has its own separate localStorage. Data saved in Chrome doesn't automatically appear in Firefox. Data on your laptop doesn't sync to your phone. If you want to use AuDHD HQ across devices, export from one and import into the other using Data Sync.
03 · Getting Started

Five steps to make it yours.

You don't have to do everything at once. These five steps are the highest-leverage starting points — the ones that make every other tool work better.

1
Set your name and theme
Go to Customize (⚙️) to enter your name. The home screen will greet you with it. You can also pick your preferred colour theme — AuDHD HQ has a time-based morning/afternoon/night theme system that shifts automatically, but you can override it.
⚙️ customize.html
2
Do your first Check-In
The Check-In is the heartbeat of AuDHD HQ. It logs your mood, energy, body state (split into Physical and Emotional sections), sleep, and workday. In the morning it also shows a quick checklist (meds, teeth, cat, water). The Notes section is a full capture panel — tag anything as a Win, Worry, Note, or Reminder and route it where it needs to go. It takes under two minutes and seeds data that appears on your home screen and in the Health Tracker's trend graphs. Do it once a day — whenever feels natural.
✅ checkin.html
3
Add one task to the Taskboard
Open the Taskboard and add something real that's on your mind — even a small one. The Taskboard has Daily, Weekly, and Monthly lanes. Your home screen shows your current Priority Stack automatically. This is how the app starts feeling useful rather than just interesting.
📋 taskboard.html
4
Explore what resonates right now
AuDHD HQ has a lot of tools. Don't try to use all of them. Look at the home screen and notice which ones feel immediately useful. Money Brain? Walking Tracker? Dream Journal? Start with what solves a current problem. The others will wait.
🏠 index.html
5
Export your data once you've added some
After you've been using the app for a few days and have data you'd be sad to lose, go to Data Sync and download a backup. Pick a folder you'll remember — a cloud folder, USB drive, or dedicated backup spot. Set a reminder to do this every couple of weeks.
🔄 data-sync.html

05 · First Week Checklist

Tiny wins, not a full deployment.

Check these off as you go. You don't have to do them in order, and you don't have to do them all in the first week. Just whenever.

  • Set my name in Customize ⚙️
  • Completed my first Check-In ✅
  • Added a task to Taskboard 📋
  • Explored the home screen and picked two tools to try this week
  • Done a Check-In three days in a row (the habit that makes everything else work)
  • Exported a data backup from Data Sync 🔄
  • Set up Health Tracker — added conditions, medications, and at least one provider in the Setup tab 🩺
  • Saved the feature guide to refer back to
  • Used Survival Mode or Firebird Protocol when I actually needed it (not just to look)
🧠
You don't have to do this perfectly.
AuDHD HQ was built to meet you where you are — including on the days when you can barely open the laptop. Use what helps. Skip what doesn't. Come back to the rest.
